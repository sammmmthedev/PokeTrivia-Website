Privacy Policy for PokéTrivia

Last updated: 7/17/2026

This privacy policy describes how PokéTrivia ("the App," "we," "us") handles information when you use it. PokéTrivia is a free, non-commercial fan project built around Pokémon trivia and party games. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by Nintendo, Game Freak, or The Pokémon Company.

Summary

PokéTrivia does not require an account, does not show ads, does not use analytics or advertising SDKs, and does not sell or share your information with third parties for marketing purposes. The only information it handles is what's needed to run a multiplayer game session and, if you choose to use voice input, to convert your spoken answer into text.

Information We Collect

Information you provide directly

  • Display name. When you host or join a Type Clash game, you enter a name of your choosing. This can be a real name, a nickname, or anything else — the App doesn't verify or require it to be your legal name.
  • Gameplay data. Round information (which Pokémon types are in play, buzz-in order, typed or spoken answers, and scores) is created as a normal part of playing the game.

Information collected automatically

  • None. The App does not collect device identifiers, advertising IDs, IP addresses, location data, or usage analytics on its own behalf. (Third parties the App connects to — described below — may log standard technical information like IP address as part of operating their own services; that's governed by their own privacy policies, not this one.)

Voice input (optional feature)

If you use the microphone button to answer by voice instead of typing, your spoken audio is processed in one of two ways depending on how this copy of the App is configured:

  • Browser-based recognition: your device's browser sends the audio directly to its own built-in speech recognition service (for example, Google's speech service in Chrome, or Apple's in Safari) to convert it to text. The App itself never receives or stores the raw audio in this case — only the resulting text.
  • Cloud-based recognition: your audio is uploaded to a server we operate (a Firebase Cloud Function) which forwards it to Google Cloud Speech-to-Text for transcription, then returns the resulting text to your device. The audio is not stored by our server; any retention of the audio by the transcription provider itself is governed by that provider's own privacy policy.

Either way, voice input is entirely optional — every answer can be typed instead, and the App works fully without ever using the microphone.

How Information Is Used

Information you provide is used solely to run the game session you're participating in: syncing game state between your device and the other players' devices in real time, keeping score, and displaying who's buzzed in. Nothing you enter is used for advertising, profiling, or any purpose unrelated to gameplay.

How Information Is Stored and Shared

Game session data (room codes, player names, scores, and round state) is stored in a Firebase Realtime Database operated by Google, so that it can sync between every device in the same game session. This data is only accessible to devices connected to that specific game room via its 6-digit code.

This data is not permanently retained — it exists only for the life of the game room. Data for a room is deleted when the host ends the room. If a host closes the app without explicitly ending the room, the room's data may persist in the database until manually cleared, though it's not accessible to anyone without that room's code.

The App does not use persistent local storage (such as browser localStorage) to remember who you are between sessions — all state exists only in memory while the App is open and is cleared when you close or reload it.

Third-Party Services

Playing PokéTrivia involves your device communicating with the following third-party services, each governed by its own privacy policy:

Service Purpose
Firebase (Google) Real-time syncing of multiplayer game state
PokéAPI Fetching Pokémon type and species data used to generate rounds and clues
pokemon.com (The Pokémon Company) Displaying official Pokémon artwork
Google Fonts Loading the App's typefaces
Google Cloud Speech-to-Text Converting spoken answers to text, if voice input is used and configured

We don't control these services' own data practices. We'd encourage reviewing their respective privacy policies if you have concerns beyond what's described here.

Children's Privacy

PokéTrivia is a general-audience trivia game and doesn't knowingly collect information from children in a way that would require special handling under laws like COPPA — mainly because the only information collected is a self-chosen display name, entered voluntarily, with no account creation or persistent identity across sessions. That said, we don't have a way to verify the age of anyone using the App. If you're a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided information through the App that concerns you, contact us using the information below and we'll assist in addressing it, including within the Firebase database directly if needed.

Data Deletion

Since there's no account system, there's no persistent profile to delete. Ending a hosted game room removes that room's data immediately. If you have concerns about data lingering in an abandoned room, contact us and we can manually remove it.

Changes to This Policy

If how the App handles information changes, this page will be updated with a new "Last updated" date. Continued use of the App after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
